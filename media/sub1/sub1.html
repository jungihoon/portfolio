Mercedes Star: Icons, Legends and Passion

Mercedes-Benz's triangle star was created in 1925, with the creation of the Mercedes-Benz brand.Check out the charming
vehicle highlights that continue to form a legendary brand to this day.

1954 | Mercedes-Benz 300 SL 'Gullwing'

In 1952, Mercedes-Benz achieved international success with the 300 SL Racing Sports Car (W194). The letter 'SL' means 'super-light' in German. Since 1954, the myth of SL has begun with the legendary '300 SL 'Gullwing', and its traces have been alive in Yangsan vehicles. Every generation continues to carry on its own traditions and constantly radiates its charm.

1963 | Mercedes-Benz 600 'Gro ß Mercedes'

The 600 model, which was introduced at the Frankfurt International Motor Show in Germany in September 1063, was a big sensation. Even the most demanding of conditions, this exclusive and new high-end model is standardized with unique technological features that represent the state-of-the-art technology the time.

1968 | Mercedes-Benz 'Strich-Acht'

Four-cylinder models 200 D through 220 were included in the model series W 115, while the six-cylinder models 230 and 250 were designated as the W 114 model series. The "new generation" model since its release in 1968 is still called the "Strich-Acht model" to this day.

1972 | Mercedes-Benz S-Class 116 Model Series

In September 1972, a completely new luxury class vehicle was opened to the public. The vehicle, first officially called the Mercedes-Benz S-Class and internally called the W 116, took over the 108/109 model series and initially consisted of 280 S, 280 SE and 350 SE models.

1977 | Mercedes-Benz 123 Model Estate

In January 1976, the new mid-range generation (W 123) was launched in a variety of ways and customers who ordered had to wait up to a year for the vehicle due to high demand for some time. Following this sedan, the first factory-built long wheel-based Estate Coupe was launched in 1977.

1979 | Mercedes-Benz G-Class

In February 1979, with the announcement of the Mercedes Benz G model, it entered a whole new field. Meanwhile, the new generation of off-road vehicles is designed as a proprietary mode of transportation with leisure characteristics, while also for everyday use in demanding industries or cities.

1982 | Mercedes-Benz 190 E

The launch of compact sedans in 190 and 190E models on December 8, 1982, was a long-awaited event for the public and industry media. The two models, which did not replace the existing models, were added as the third major line to the existing Mercedes-Benz passenger car collection. This new model series features more compact dimensions, lightweights and affordability based on drivability, safety and reliability.
